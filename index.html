Core Concepts

About The Project

Leveraging predictive neural patterns to assist cardiologists in assessing coronary anomalies before critical complications arise.

Problem Statement

Cardiovascular diseases represent the leading cause of global mortality. Conventional diagnostics often suffer from human visual margins of error and processing constraints when resolving non-linear combinations of complex physiological variables.

Objectives

To construct a highly responsive, binary predictive framework capable of categorizing cardiovascular risk profiles while providing transparent medical explanations, helping clinicians expedite emergency triaging loops.

Binary Classification

The neural platform computes risk outcomes using binary logs: Class 0 (Normal cardiac baseline, low-risk markers) and Class 1 (Indication of coronary narrowing exceeding 50%, high risk, immediate critical clinical path).

Role of AI

AI resolves multi-dimensional correlations (e.g. cholesterol coefficients interacting with ST slopes and thal baselines) that are hard to capture during typical clinical visual interpretations.

Clinical Benefits

Decreases initial evaluation timelines by up to 40%, automates preventative triage, increases diagnostic accuracy coefficients, and lowers cardiac complications through earlier preventative care.

Hospital Integration

The platform sits within the clinical triage pipeline, connecting patient intake data directly to machine learning risk calculations.

Data Preprocessing

Data Cleaning Timeline

How raw medical measurements are cleaned and optimized before ingestion into predictive neural models.

Step 1

Duplicate Removal

Discovered 723 redundant matching records inside the raw dataset. Removing duplicate rows guarantees that validation subsets remain statistically isolated and avoids artificial inflation of training accuracies.

df.drop_duplicates(inplace=True)
Step 2

Outlier Treatment

Applied the Interquartile Range (IQR) method to filter physiological anomalies (e.g. cholesterol values exceeding 450 mg/dl and blood pressure levels above 180 mmHg) that represent non-standard medical baselines.

Q1, Q3 = df['chol'].quantile([0.25, 0.75])
Step 3

One-Hot Encoding

Transformed multi-class categorical arrays (such as chest pain type `cp` and resting electrocardiographic results `restecg`) into single-column binary indexes to prevent the algorithm from assuming false ordinal rankings.

pd.get_dummies(df, columns=['cp', 'restecg'])
Step 4

MinMax Scaling

Normalized continuous vectors (e.g. cholesterol, age, max heart rate achieved) within boundaries of 0 and 1. This prevents variables with large integers from dominating weight coefficients.

MinMaxScaler().fit_transform(X)

Feature Engineering

Applying clinical domain expertise to synthesize new mathematical parameters, improving predictive accuracy and classification margins.

Age Grouping

Purpose: Segments patient ages into discrete life decades to align with age-related vascular hardening thresholds.

Clinical Meaning: Cardiovascular risk curves display severe non-linear spikes after the fifth decade of life.

Age_Group = age // 10

BP Category

Purpose: Categorizes resting blood pressure values into specific clinical hypertension ranges.

Clinical Meaning: Distinguishes normal pressure from severe Stage 2 hypertensive stress on arterial walls.

Bins: [0, 120, 140, 160, 300]

Cholesterol Category

Purpose: Segments serum cholesterol counts matching AHA cardiological safety guidelines.

Clinical Meaning: Separates optimal lipoprotein levels from high-risk lipid accumulation thresholds.

Bins: [0, 200, 240, 600]

Cardiac Risk Score

Purpose: Builds an additive baseline index using binary risk indicators from clinical tests.

Clinical Meaning: Represents cumulative diagnostic indicators pointing towards coronary vascular narrowing.

Score = sum(exang + (ca>0) + (oldpeak>1.5))

Age × Heart Rate

Purpose: Analyzes the interaction of age and max cardiovascular capacity during physical stress.

Clinical Meaning: Elevated combinations in older patients indicate high stress and reduced coronary capacity.

Age_HR_Interaction = age * thalach

Oldpeak Log

Purpose: Log-normalizes right-skewed ST depression values to stabilize classification weights.

Clinical Meaning: Compresses high-value outlier variances to avoid bias in logistic models.

Oldpeak_Log = log(oldpeak + 1)
